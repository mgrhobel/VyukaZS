**A6 SUPPLEMENT**

**Using the Telephone (*****face2face*** **Unit 7D)**

\

**Answering the phone**

Most textbooks say that, when answering the phone, British say "Hello"
and then give their telephone number. However, this is not done so much
anymore. In a formal situation, for example, a business office, the
person answering would say "Hello" and then give the name of the
business and/or their own name when answering. For personal use,
Americans just say "Hello"; they never give the number. For businesses,
etc, it is the same as with British. When answering a mobile phone ring,
just say "Hello." In any case, the caller then responds by saying:
"Hello. This is Jana Novakova." "Hello. This is Jana." Or "Hi. It's me."
**Note**: Unless you and the person you are calling are very close,
never assume they will recognize your voice or know [which]{.underline}
Jana you are. Always give your last name. \[Can use Good morning, Good
afternoon & Good evening, but rather formal.\]

\

**Signaling readiness to end the call**

\

People usually signal that they are ready to end the call by slowing
their responses and using a word like "Well,...." followed by a comment
indicating they want to "wind up" (finish) the call.

E.g.: Well, I guess I'd better get going. I have a lot to do. Right,
well, I'll give you a call next Monday.

\

People who are socially sensitive, "pick up on" (notice and recognize)
this signal and help to bring the call to an end. People who are not
socially sensitive are the ones you can not get off the phone.

\

**Saying "goodbye"**

\

British and Americans say: Goodbye (all-purpose), bye, bye-bye, see you
(later), see ya, talk to you soon.

("Bye" usually last.) Often a combination: E.g. Well, see ya later. Bye.

British also say "ta-ra" very informally. (spelling: goodbye or
good-bye)

\

If you want, before saying goodbye, you can say something like: Have a
nice day. Have a nice weekend. Etc. Say: "Have a nice time" ONLY if the
person is going to a social event, like a party, an entertainment event,
like a concert, or a holiday/vacation. What you then mean is: Have a
nice time at the party. Have a nice time at the concert. Have a nice
time on your vacation (in Italy).Or Have a nice time at your grandma's
house this weekend. (Note: Both British and Americans say: "hang up."
British sometimes say: "ring off.")

\

**Reacting to problems on the phone**

\

**Text**

(Usually because of bad connection/bad line.)

There's a bit of a delay on the line.

Sorry, you're breaking up a bit.

I didn't catch all of that.

Sorry, it's a bad line.

You'll have to speak up a bit. (Voice too low, bad connection or too
noisy to hear properly.)

The reception isn't very good here.

Sorry, I didn't get any of that. (Voice too low, bad connection or too
noisy)

I keep losing you.

Sorry, we got cut off.

\

I'm just about to run out of credit.

I think my battery's about to run out.

**Extra**

\

There seems to be a bit of a delay on the line.

There seems to be a problem with this line/connection.

Sorry, I couldn't/didn't catch/hear/get what you said./...some/any of
what you said.

It's a bad connection. We've got a bad connection. Seems like we have a
bad connection.

You're fading out. You're fading in and out.

I'm having trouble hearing you. Please speak up a bit./ Please speak a
bit louder.

Could/would/can you repeat what you just said? I couldn't/can't hear you
(properly).

Would/could/can you repeat that, please?

I can't hear you (clearly/well/here).

It's too noisy. It's a bad line. It's a bad connection.

\

**Dealing with the problem**

\

**Text**

\

Shall I call you back on your landline? (BE) (AE: Should/can I call you
back.....?

Would you like me to phone you back?

Do you want me to give you a ring later? (AE: ...give you a call later?)

\

**Extra**

\

Let me call you back (in a minute/right away/straight away/later/etc.).

Can I call you back on your landline/in a few minutes/this
afternoon/etc.?

Would you like me to phone/call you back (in a few minutes/later)?

Can I give you a ring/call later? (Note: Americans don't usually use
"ring.")

Let me call/phone/ring you back (in a minute/later).

Let me hang up and try again.

I'll call/phone you right back./I'll call you back in a few minutes/in
10 minutes/etc.

Let me get to/find a quieter place (so I can hear you/so I can talk
better).

\
