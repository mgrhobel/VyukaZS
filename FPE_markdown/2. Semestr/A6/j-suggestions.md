**A6 SUPPLEMENT -- MAKING SUGGESTIONS**

face2face 9D

\

There are many kinds of suggestions, made in all sorts of situations.
This lesson focuses only on suggestions about what friends or colleagues
might do together for fun or entertainment in their leisure time. They
differ in terms of whether the person making the suggestion asserts
his/her preference immediately, makes a first suggestion which is more
general and specifics must be decided mutually (Let's do something
tonight.), or suggests alternatives and asks the other to decide.
Responses can be immediate agreement, a clear choice of one alternative,
an assertion that it doesn't matter (and the other should decide), or a
rejection of the suggestion(s) altogether (e.g. Sorry, I can't do
anything tonight. Or: I'm too busy (to go out tonight).

\

**Asking if the person is free**

\

*Text*

Are you doing anything (this evening)?

Have you got anything on (this Saturday)? (BE)

What are you up to (on Sunday)?

\

*Extra*

Are you free Friday night/this weekend/etc.?

Do you have time to go (out to eat sometime this week/for a coffee this
afternoon)?

\

**Making a suggestion**

\

*Text*

I thought we could give (that new club) a try.

I wouldn't mind (going to that). How about you?

Do you feel like (having an Indian)? (BE)

Do you fancy (going to hear them play)? (BE)

\

*Extra*

Let's do something (tonight/this weekend)

Let's go (out tonight/to the.....)

How about going (to.....) with me tonight?

Want to go for a walk sometime this weekend/Saturday morning?

I'm up for (a film tonight). How about you?

\

*Asserting one's own preference*:

I think we should......

I'd (really) like to......Do you want to go/go with me/join me/come with
me?

\

**Politely refusing/rejecting a suggestion**

\

*Text*

I'm sorry, but I don't feel up to (going to a club tonight).

Some other time, perhaps. (Sounds rather formal)

I'd rather give (that) a miss, if you don't mind. (BE)

\

*Extra*

Sorry, I've already got (something planned/another date/something else
to do) this weekend.

Maybe another/some other time. I'm too busy this week.

Sorry, I'm too busy to go out tonight. Maybe next week. I'll give you a
call./Give me a call.

Well, actually, I have too much to do tonight. I have to stay home and
work.

Oh, I don't know. I don't really feel like going out tonight.

I think I'd rather stay in tonight/work on my project/stay home and
watch TV.

\

Actually, I'd prefer a pizza tonight.

To tell the truth, I don't much care for/like Chinese food.

\

**Suggesting alternatives**

\

Do you feel like Chinese or Italian food?

Are you up for Czech traditional or Italian (food)?

Would you prefer to (see this film) or (that film)?

Would you rather (go to the concert) or (catch a film)?

Do you want to (stay home and watch a DVD) or (go to the cinema)?

We could (take a walk) or (stay home for the night). Which would you
prefer/rather do?

Which film do you want to see, (this one) or (that one)?

\

**Saying you have no preference**

\

*Text*

I'm easy. Whatever you like.

I really don't mind. It's up to you. (BE)

I'm not bothered either way. (BE)

It's all the same to me.

\

*Extra*

I (really) don't care (either way). (AE)

It doesn't matter to me. You choose/decide.

I really don't have a preference. It's up to you.

Whatever you want (to do) is fine with me.

I'm too tired to think about it! You decide.

Either one is OK by me.

\

**Speaking of final arrangements**

\

Let me find out the details and get back to you.

I'll let you know (what time it starts) when I see you (tomorrow).

I'm not sure what time it starts. I'll find out and let you know.

I'll call you (tomorrow/later) and tell you (what time it starts/ what
time I'm free).

I'll get back to you about the details.

\

**Note**: these words/phrases can be followed by verb+ing:

...wouldn't mind (going)/feel like.../ fancy.../feel up to....

...prefer...

\

**Other contexts for suggestions**:

\

Business decisions, professional (work-related) decisions, academic
decisions, political decisions,

financial decisions (including purchases), career decisions, travel
decisions, personal decisions (relationships, etc.) Some situations
involve advice given by a friend, family member, colleague or
professional advisor.

(I think you should...../ If I were you, I would.....)

\

*Introducing suggestions*:

\

More formal: I suggest (that) we (do something). / I suggest (doing
something).

\

More informal: Why don't we/you (do s/t)? / How about (doing s/t)? /
Let's (do s/t).

\

\

**Sample dialogues**

\

Do you want to go out to eat?

OK, sure. Where do you want to go?

I really don't care. It's up to you.

OK, how about Chinese?

Fine with me. What time?

We can go at 6.00 or later. What would you prefer?

Later is better for me. How about 7.00?

OK. I'll meet you there about 7.00.

\

How about a film tonight?

OK, sure. Do you mean to watch a DVD at home or go to the cinema?

I'd rather go out. How about you?

That's fine with me.

\

We could go to the new pizza place or the Chinese restaurant.

Doesn't matter to me./ Either one is OK with me.

OK, then. Let's go to/try the pizza place.

Fine. I'll get my coat and let's go.

\

Would you like to go to that concert tonight?

Sure, great! What time should we meet? And where?

Why don't we meet in front of the concert hall about 7.30?

OK, fine. See you there.
